The Problem

Stop wasting months on fragmented classifieds

Industrial procurement in New Zealand is broken. Sourcing specialist equipment means months of emails, opaque dealer pricing, and international freight gambles — while your production capacity sits idle waiting.

🔍

No Centralised Market

Equipment is scattered across Trade Me, dealer websites, international platforms, and word-of-mouth networks with no unified, searchable database.

💰

Zero Price Transparency

Capital-constrained SMEs have no benchmark pricing, leaving them vulnerable to overcharging or buying the wrong specification entirely.

🌍

Import Complexity

Direct importing from China or Europe takes months and offers no local warranty, NZ compliance verification, or maintenance support.

The Solution

What market.equipA delivers

Purpose-built features that directly address the friction points in this layer of New Zealand's industrial ecosystem.

✅

Verified Listings

Every listing is verified — new, used, and refurbished machinery from NZ's leading dealers, hire fleets, and institutions in one searchable database.

📊

Transparent Pricing

Benchmark pricing and comparable listings so you buy with confidence, not guesswork. Know what the market actually pays.

🤝

Direct to Seller

Connect directly with NZ equipment dealers, plant hire operators, and institutional sellers — no intermediary friction or commission layers.

🔧

Linked to care.equipA

Every listing connects to certified local technicians, service history, and calibration records so you know exactly what you're getting.

Porter's 5 Forces

The competitive landscape in NZ

A structural analysis of the market forces shaping this layer of the equipA.kiwi ecosystem.

🏆
Rivalry
High

Trade Me and global platforms exist but lack niche industrial UX, verified listings, and NZ-specific compliance and logistics data.

🏭
Supplier Power
Medium

NZ dealers hold inventory but need better digital reach to move stock faster and access a wider, qualified buyer pool.

💼
Buyer Power
High

Capital-constrained NZ SMEs are highly price-sensitive and will shift to whichever platform delivers the best data and lowest friction.

🔄
Substitution
Medium

Direct importing and traditional dealer networks remain viable but are slow, high-risk, and offer no post-sale support.

🚪
New Entrants
Low

Building a verified full-stack industrial marketplace requires deep trust, logistics knowledge, and NZ-specific institutional relationships.

PESTLE Analysis

Key macro factors driving this layer

The political, economic, social, technological, legal, and environmental forces that make this module strategically critical right now.

E

High interest rates make refurbished gear far more attractive than new imports — demand for a trusted secondary market has never been higher.

T

AI-powered categorisation and metadata enrichment solves the chaos of inconsistent equipment descriptions across thousands of listings.

P

Government 'buy NZ' procurement reform and circular economy targets actively favour domestic secondary market platforms.
